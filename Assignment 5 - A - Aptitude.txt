1) A and B take part in a 100 m race. A runs at 5 km/hr. A gives B a start of 8 m and still beats him by 8 secs. The speed of B is:

Now, calculate the time A takes to run 100 m:
Time taken by A = Distance / Speed = 100 m / (25/18) m/s = 72 seconds

Since A beats B by 8 seconds, B takes 72 + 8 = 80 seconds to cover 100 m.

Now, calculate B's speed:

Convert B's speed to km/hr:

Let's first find the speed of A, B, and C in the 100 m race:
Speed of A = 100 m / Time taken by A
Speed of B = 100 m / Time taken by B
Speed of C = 100 m / Time taken by C

Then, the time taken by B will be (t + 10) seconds, and the time taken by C will be (t + 13) seconds.

Now, calculate the speeds:

Speed of B = 100 m / (t + 10)
